⚔ Parry

A dark-fantasy tabletop RPG of duels, guard-breaks, and hard-won victories. Every roll is an exchange — you and your foe both throw dice, and the gap between them decides everything.

The idea

Most systems ask "did you hit?" Parry asks how well — and then hands you that answer as currency to spend. Beat their guard by a hair and you land a glancing blow. Beat it soundly and you can wound them, disarm them, or drive them off a ledge. Read their swing perfectly and your blades bind, turning the fight on its head.

It takes its ethos from Souls-likes: menacing foes, deliberate combat, greed that gets punished — and the feeling that when the monster finally falls, you earned it.

The core loop

1 · Roll against each other

The attacker rolls d20 + attribute + weapon. Lesser foes have a static defense value; named foes and bosses roll their own defense against you. The difference between the two numbers is the result.

2 · Spend the margin

Margin over their defensePoints earned
1–4 — a glancing blow1 point
5–9 — a solid hit2 points
10+ — a devastating blow3 points

Spend points on Strike for damage — or on maneuvers: Shove, Opening, Feint, Disarm, Trip, Drive Back, Sunder, Rally, or a lasting Wound. One roll, and a real decision every single time.

3 · Press your luck

Roll even and your side keeps acting. Roll odd and the spotlight flips to the enemy — whether you hit or not. Every extra swing is a gamble, and the opposition's turn is paid for out of the actions you took to get there.

4 · Break their guard

Bosses don't die by attrition. Pressure fills their Poise; when it breaks they're staggered — guard open, taking double damage, while your party unloads a burst of uninterruptible actions. Break the giant, then punish it.

The Perfect Parry. When both dice come up the same natural number, the blow is turned and initiative snaps back to the defender's side. Against a boss, it shatters their guard outright.
