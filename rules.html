Rules

Everything that happens in a fight, from the first swing to the last breath.

The Opposed Roll

When a character attacks, they roll d20 + ability modifier + weapon Atk. The GM then does one of two things:

  • Minor threats have a static defense value, compared directly against the attacker's roll.
  • Named foes and bosses roll their own defense — d20 + ability + weapon Def + shield + armor — against the attacker's roll.

If the defense value is higher, the attack misses. Otherwise, compare the difference.

A Perfect Parry. When the natural values of both dice are identical, the attack is parried — it deals no damage and applies no effects. Initiative immediately swings to the defender's side, regardless of how many actions the other side had banked, and that defender must take the next action. Against a boss, a Perfect Parry also fills their Poise gauge instantly, staggering them.

Openings & Setups

No roll should ever be wasted. When a character attacks and misses, the target is considered distracted blocking that blow, and takes a penalty to their defense equal to the attack modifier of the character who missed.

A Setup lasts only until the next action is taken — used or not — then it's gone. It rewards immediate follow-up, encourages teamwork, and costs almost nothing to track.

Movement

A character may move once each turn for free, up to their movement speed. Moving again requires an Athletics test of 16 or higher; on a success you move again — and add an action to the opposition's pool.

Driven into obstacles. If you are moved forcefully and collide with a solid, immovable object, you take 1 Grit for every 10 ft you were unable to travel in that direction.

Attributes

Characters begin with five points distributed across six attributes. No attribute may exceed 3 at level one. Each point grants +1 to rolls using that attribute.

Grit, Armor & Wounds

Grit is your health — a track of 5 + Willpower. Fill it and you are downed, and must immediately make a Death Move.

Armor Slots come from your armor and shield. Spend one reactively to reduce an incoming injury by one tier. They're recovered through rests and repairs.

Wounds are lasting harm. Each Wound you carry lowers your maximum Grit by 1 until it is treated, on top of whatever functional effect it inflicts.

Poise — Breaking a Boss

Menacing creatures have a strong guard and take persistent pressure to open up. This is their Poise Gauge. Landing an attack raises it:

  • Dealing Grit — 1 point of Poise per point of Grit dealt.
  • Moving the target — 1 point of Poise per 10 ft moved.

Armor still counts: even when a boss reduces Grit with Armor Slots, the blow still raises their Poise — armor delays the damage, not the guard-break.

Staggered. When the gauge fills, the boss's guard is open until it takes its next turn — and it must spend that entire turn recovering, resetting Poise to zero. While staggered it takes double damage, and the players gain a number of actions equal to their party size, divided as they choose, which cannot shift initiative even when rolled with Fear.

A boss's Poise gauge also decreases by 1 each time initiative shifts back to them — let up, and they recover their footing.
